TOURING

Miami Design District

The Miami Design District is located just north of downtown. It's recognized for its excellent high-end shopping and eating scene, with Prada, Saint Laurent, and Dior boutiques as well as chic eateries spread throughout. Art galleries, antique merchants, designer stores, and gleaming showrooms thrive in this beautiful area. The Design District is also home to plenty of creative services and architectural enterprises.

Wynwood Walls

This huge outdoor show includes a selection of street art. The exhibition has more than 50 artists from all around the world, including those from Miami. The six major buildings, each of which was changed from a warehouse to a canvas and finally to a work of art, are all within walking distance.

Jungle Island

Jungle Island is a fantastic eco-adventure park located between downtown and South Beach in Miami. It has beautiful green spaces, a gorgeous beach, and stunning views of the city's skyline, in addition to its great animal displays, exhibits, and interactions. Among the species on exhibit are capybaras, lemurs, sloths, e xotic birds, giraffes, and gorillas.  You may also try out ziplines, climbing walls, and escape rooms.

Lincoln Road

Apart from the famous Ocean Drive, Lincoln Road, which passes through the centre of the city, is the primary shopping and dining destination in Miami Beach. The popular walking street, which is surrounded by amazing architecture with palms, fountains, and flowers, has a very vibrant air because of all the people that go there.

Downtown Miami

Downtown Miami is a lively commercial district with a variety of cultural and entertainment facilities. During the day, people fill Bayside, an open-aired mall, while at night, fans go to the FTX Arena for Miami Heat basketball games and concerts of famous singers. The Miami City Ballet and Florida Grand Opera perform in the Adrienne Arsht Center, while the Pérez Art Museum Miami exhibits modern art.
